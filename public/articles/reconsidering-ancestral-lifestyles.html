Reconsidering the Trend of Embracing Ancestral Lifestyles: A Call for Moral Values

In the digital age, the internet overflows with self-proclaimed gurus advocating a return to our ancestral roots. They suggest walking barefoot, taking cold showers, and adopting a carnivorous diet as paths to better health and well-being. While these practices undoubtedly offer certain benefits, it’s essential to scrutinize the concept of labeling them as “ancestor-like” activities.

While some argue for the health advantages of consuming meat, it’s important to acknowledge that abstaining from meat can also yield substantial benefits. For the animals involved, the story takes a darker turn as they suffer in the process. This ethical dilemma questions the morality of our dietary choices in the modern world.

Let’s circle back to the initial point – the terminology. Branding these activities as “ancestor-like” implies that we faithfully emulate our forebears. In reality, those who embrace such practices selectively cherry-pick elements that appeal to them from ancient lifestyles. Not all ancient humans consumed meat exclusively; many thrived on plant-based diets, benefiting from the abundance of available vegetation and minimizing the risks associated with hunting.

Additionally, scavenging provided a less perilous method of calorie acquisition. However, today’s proponents of ancestral living wouldn’t dream of dining on roadkill. Thus, we should reevaluate the accuracy of this label.

Instead, we should refer to these activities as potential contributors to personal health and well-being, without tying them exclusively to our ancestors. These practices might align with some of their habits, but they don’t encompass the full spectrum of practices that benefit our modern lives.

It’s crucial to shift our perspective on this topic. Embracing an ancient lifestyle isn’t a direct endorsement of our ancestors’ moral values. They adhered to norms and practices that might not align with our current ethical standards. If we wish to mimic their lifestyles, we should be prepared to embrace aspects like conflict, sexual aggression, abandonment, and ruthlessness, which were part of their lives as well.

In essence, we have the liberty to label these practices as we see fit. What truly matters is our ability to discern and select elements from our ancestral past that resonate with improving our lives, well-being, and society while upholding ethical values that abstain from consuming animals. This approach allows us to craft a lifestyle that best aligns with our individual values, without making blanket claims about the superiority of ancient ways.

In the end, our pursuit should be grounded in moral values, not simply ancient traditions. While we can draw inspiration from our ancestors, our ultimate goal should be to create a lifestyle that is rooted in modern ethics and conducive to our personal growth and well-being.
